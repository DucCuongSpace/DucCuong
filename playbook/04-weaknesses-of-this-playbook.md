# Weaknesses of this playbook

1. Using humans to fix AI writing makes it hard to grow fast.
The plan illustrate humans must heavily edit everything to keep it high-quality. However, if humans have to rewrite every AI draft, you can’t produce a huge amount of content quickly without hiring a massive team.

2. The plan assumes Google’s rules won’t change.
The strategy is based on how Google works right now. It doesn't have a backup plan for when Google changes its search engine in the future, which could make today’s tactics stop working or even get you penalized.

3. It requires too much money and expert skill.
The system uses multiple expensive AI tools and needs highly skilled editors. This is too costly and complicated for small businesses or teams with small budgets to actually use.

4. The "Two-AI" idea hasn't actually been proven to work.
The idea of using one AI to write and a second AI to check the work sounds good, but there is no proof it actually helps. I don't know if that extra step is worth the time and money it takes.
